Dear all,

We would like to draw your attention to a recent eLife survey open until December 2nd, which focuses on the experiences of people who know and help researchers with mental health difficulties. They are particularly interested in hearing from early-career researchers, PIs and non-academic staff who support or have supported peers and trainees.

Go to survey

As you may be aware, there are growing concerns about *mental health in academia, for staff and graduate students*. Less is known, however, about the needs of those who may be informally supporting academics who struggle. This, in turn, limits what can be done to assist supporters in their role.

The survey aims to help fill that gap. Please consider taking part, as the more they hear from people in supporting roles, the better they can inform the design of resources tailored to supporters' needs. We'd also be grateful if you could share the survey with your colleagues, your labs, and within your institution. You can forward this email, share this tweet and repost this on Facebook.

If you are curious to hear from people who have supported academics, the articles in this collection may be relevant. I’ll leave you with two quotes from one of the features:

“I wouldn't quite say my lab mates helped keep me alive, that's over exaggerating. But they certainly kept me going.” (from a postdoc)

“Many early and mid-career PIs recognize the importance of mental wellbeing for our students in part based on our own experiences having, or having had challenges with mental wellbeing.” (from a PI)
