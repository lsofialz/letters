Harassment in Academia

Harassment is the repeated and demeaning mistreatment of someone, usually led by Power abuse. Actions leading to harassment can be intentional or not, and leave the victim hurt, anxious, humiliated and feeling worthless. Incidents of bullying or harassment in Academia are astonishingly common, as one third of academics report they have been bullied in the past year and around 40% say they have witnessed it. This is three times higher than in general work place.

Harassment is not a normal cause of stress in our PhD

Harassment leads to extremely high levels of stress and will most likely impact the mental and physical health of the victims, besides eroding creativity and well-being of the whole team.

This form of stress is independent of the victims' behaviour, should not be normalised and requires a different mode of action than coping with the previously mentioned causes of stress in our PhDs.

Breaking the cycle

If this resonates with you: this is not your fault. Bullies take advantage of their power position to degrade your self-esteem and make you believe so, but you didn't do anything to deserve being harassed. Our PhD and career shouldn't grow at the expense of our mental health. We shouldn´t cope with this type of stress if it is undermining yourself. You are worth more than that. Also, the fastest you take action, the fastest it will stop.

Take care of yourself

  1. Take care of your yourself first - visit the counsellor for free click here.
  2. Document everything that is happening: who, when, how and witnesses.
  3. If you are not in safety, visit a doctor to help you (for eg. getting a sick leave until you are back on your feet).

Take action

We strongly encourage you to take action to stop the bully. For that, contact Michaela Herzig from PhD office, Christiane Nollte from the Equality office or Ingo from the Staff council. They are all extremely professional, keep confidentiality, speak english and german and will help you along the way. If you did this already, note that the MDC changed recently and everyone is more aware of this problem. You are fully entitled to ask for a behavioural change from the perpetrator. Write us for support!

What is being done at the MDC?

Currently there is a task force (intranet link) composed by several groups of employees including us PhD reps, deciding on a code of conduct for the MDC as well as clear procedures in case of harassment, which end up with consequences for the perpetrators. Our goal is to make the MDC a safe environment to work and we have the support of the Directors for this. These measures will be implemented in the next 6 months.
